# Business Model Canvas — 商业模式画布

Generate a complete business model analysis using the Business Model Canvas framework. Cover all 9 building blocks with specific, actionable content.

## When to Use

- User asks for business model analysis or canvas
- User needs to evaluate a startup or product idea
- User wants to prepare for investor pitches
- User wants to compare business models across competitors
- User asks "how does this make money"

## The 9 Building Blocks

### 1. Value Propositions
What unique value do we deliver? Which customer problems do we solve? Which needs do we satisfy? What bundles of products/services?

### 2. Customer Segments
Who are our most important customers? Mass market / Niche / Segmented / Diversified / Multi-sided platform. For each segment: demographics, JTBD, willingness to pay.

### 3. Channels
How do we reach customers? Awareness → Evaluation → Purchase → Delivery → After-sales. Direct vs indirect. Channel efficiency.

### 4. Customer Relationships
What relationship does each segment expect? Personal assistance / Self-service / Automated / Communities / Co-creation. Acquisition vs retention cost.

### 5. Revenue Streams
What are customers truly willing to pay for? Transaction / Recurring / Usage fees / Subscription / Licensing / Advertising. Revenue by segment.

### 6. Key Resources
What assets are required? Physical / Intellectual / Human / Financial. Core vs support resources.

### 7. Key Activities
What must we do exceptionally well? Production / Problem-solving / Platform management. Core differentiators.

### 8. Key Partnerships
Who are key partners and suppliers? Strategic alliances / Joint ventures / Buyer-supplier relationships. What to outsource.

### 9. Cost Structure
Most important costs? Cost-driven vs value-driven. Fixed vs variable costs. Economies of scale/scope.

## Output Format

Present as a structured analysis with all 9 blocks, each containing 2-4 specific bullet points. End with a 2-3 sentence synthesis of the core business engine.

## Alternative Frameworks

- **Lean Canvas**: For early-stage startups (replaces Partners with Problem, Resources with Solution, adds Unfair Advantage)
- **Platform Model**: For marketplaces (adds Network Effects, Chicken-and-Egg analysis)

## Pitfalls

- Don't use generic placeholders like "high quality" or "great service" — be specific
- Don't skip revenue streams — every business model MUST explain how money flows
- For multi-sided platforms, analyze each side separately

Based on Alexander Osterwalder's Business Model Canvas methodology.
