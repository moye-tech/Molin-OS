    15|## Competitive Battlecard
    16|
    17|Create a concise, sales-ready battlecard for use against a specific competitor.
    18|
    19|### Context
    20|
    21|You are creating a competitive battlecard for **$ARGUMENTS**.
    22|
    23|Use web search to research the competitor's current product, pricing, positioning, and recent changes. If the user provides files (feature lists, win/loss data, sales call notes), read them first.
    24|
    25|### Instructions
    26|
    27|1. **Research the competitor** (use web search):
    28|   - Current product offerings and features
    29|   - Pricing tiers and model
    30|   - Target market and positioning
    31|   - Recent product launches or changes
    32|   - Known strengths and weaknesses
    33|   - Customer reviews and sentiment (G2, Capterra, Reddit)
    34|
    35|2. **Create the battlecard** with these sections:
    36|
    37|   ### Company Overview
    38|   - Founded, HQ, funding/revenue (if public)
    39|   - Target market and ICP
    40|   - Positioning in one sentence
    41|
    42|   ### Quick Comparison
    43|
    44|   | Capability | Us | Them | Winner |
    45|   |---|---|---|---|
    46|   | [Feature area 1] | [Our approach] | [Their approach] | [Us/Them/Tie] |
    47|   | [Feature area 2] | ... | ... | ... |
    48|   | Pricing | ... | ... | ... |
    49|   | Support | ... | ... | ... |
    50|
    51|   ### Where We Win
    52|   - [Advantage 1]: [Proof point or customer quote]
    53|   - [Advantage 2]: [Specific capability they lack]
    54|   - [Advantage 3]: [Better approach with reasoning]
    55|
    56|   ### Where They Win
    57|   - [Their strength 1]: [Our counter-positioning]
    58|   - [Their strength 2]: [How we mitigate this gap]
    59|
    60|   ### Common Objections & Responses
    61|
    62|   | Prospect Says | Respond With |
    63|   |---|---|
    64|   | "Competitor X has [feature]" | "[Our alternative approach and why it's better for them]" |
    65|   | "They're cheaper" | "[Value framing: total cost of ownership, ROI, hidden costs]" |
    66|   | "They're more established" | "[Our advantages: speed, innovation, focus, support]" |
    67|
    68|   ### Landmines to Plant
    69|   Questions to ask the prospect that highlight competitor weaknesses:
    70|   - "How important is [area where we excel] to your team?"
    71|   - "Have you evaluated [specific capability they lack]?"
    72|
    73|   ### Win/Loss Patterns
    74|   - We tend to win when: [pattern]
    75|   - We tend to lose when: [pattern]
    76|   - Key differentiator in competitive deals: [what tips the scale]
    77|
    78|3. **Keep it scannable**: Sales reps need to reference this during calls. Use tables, bold text, and short bullets.
    79|
    80|Save as markdown. Format for easy printing or sharing in Notion/Confluence.
