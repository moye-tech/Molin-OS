    14|# GTM Motions
    15|
    16|## Overview
    17|Identify and evaluate the best go-to-market motions for your product. This skill analyzes seven proven GTM approaches with specific tools and tactics to help you build a balanced acquisition strategy.
    18|
    19|## When to Use
    20|- Selecting marketing channels for your product
    21|- Choosing between inbound vs outbound strategy
    22|- Building your GTM toolkit and tech stack
    23|- Evaluating PLG vs traditional sales motion
    24|- Planning cross-channel marketing campaigns
    25|
    26|## The 7 GTM Motions
    27|
    28|### 1. Inbound Marketing
    29|Attract customers through valuable content and thought leadership.
    30|- **Tools**: LinkedIn, SEMRush, Grammarly, HubSpot, Airtable
    31|- **Tactics**: Blog content, webinars, whitepapers, SEO, email nurture sequences
    32|- **Best For**: B2B SaaS, technical products, long sales cycles
    33|- **Strength**: Builds brand authority and attracts high-intent prospects
    34|- **Challenge**: Requires consistent content creation; slower to show results
    35|
    36|### 2. Outbound Sales
    37|Proactively reach target prospects through direct engagement.
    38|- **Tools**: LinkedIn Sales Navigator, ZoomInfo, Lemlist, Apollo, Hunter
    39|- **Tactics**: Cold email campaigns, LinkedIn outreach, phone prospecting, personalized demos
    40|- **Best For**: Enterprise sales, high-value contracts, niche markets
    41|- **Strength**: Predictable pipeline generation; control over target selection
    42|- **Challenge**: Low response rates; resource-intensive; requires skilled sales team
    43|
    44|### 3. Paid Digital Advertising
    45|Reach target audiences through paid channels with precision targeting.
    46|- **Tools**: Google Ads, Meta Ads, LinkedIn Ads, Newswire, Retargeting platforms
    47|- **Tactics**: Search ads, display advertising, social ads, video advertising, retargeting
    48|- **Best For**: Products with clear target demographics, competitive keywords
    49|- **Strength**: Fast results; scalable; measurable ROI; precise targeting
    50|- **Challenge**: Can be expensive; requires continuous optimization; competitive
    51|
    52|### 4. Community Marketing
    53|Build engaged communities where customers help each other and spread the word.
    54|- **Tools**: Slack, Reddit, Discord, Circle, Mighty Networks, WhatsApp
    55|- **Tactics**: Community forums, user groups, events, mentorship, ambassador programs
    56|- **Best For**: Developer products, communities of practice, loyal user bases
    57|- **Strength**: Builds loyalty; organic word-of-mouth; valuable feedback; low CAC
    58|- **Challenge**: Requires active moderation; time to build critical mass
    59|
    60|### 5. Partner Marketing
    61|Leverage partner networks to co-market and reach new audiences.
    62|- **Tools**: Miro, AWS Startups, Oracle Partners, Stripe, Shopify App Store
    63|- **Tactics**: Partner integrations, co-marketing agreements, channel partnerships, resellers
    64|- **Best For**: Complementary products, platform ecosystems, expanding market reach
    65|- **Strength**: Access to established customer bases; shared costs; credibility
    66|- **Challenge**: Partner alignment; revenue sharing; dependency on partners
    67|
    68|### 6. Account-Based Marketing (ABM)
    69|Treat high-value accounts as individual markets with personalized campaigns.
    70|- **Tools**: Pipedrive, Hunter, Clay, 6sense, Terminus, Demandbase
    71|- **Tactics**: Personalized messaging, account-targeted content, coordinated sales/marketing
    72|- **Best For**: Enterprise deals, limited target accounts, high deal values
    73|- **Strength**: Higher conversion rates; larger deal sizes; strong sales-marketing alignment
    74|- **Challenge**: Requires detailed account research; resource intensive; not scalable to SMB
    75|
    76|### 7. Product-Led Growth (PLG)
    77|Drive adoption through the product experience itself with minimal sales friction.
    78|- **Tools**: Hotjar, Amplitude, Sentry, PostHog, Intercom, Appcues
    79|- **Tactics**: Free trials, freemium models, in-app onboarding, self-serve demos, product analytics
    80|- **Best For**: Self-service products, SMB market, low ACV, viral potential
    81|- **Strength**: Low CAC; aligns product and growth; strong PMF signals; scalable
    82|- **Challenge**: Requires excellent product experience; lower price points; longer ROI
    83|
    84|## How It Works
    85|
    86|### Step 1: Understand Your Product
    87|Define product characteristics:
    88|- Price point and ACV (contract value)
    89|- Sales cycle length
    90|- Buyer type and decision-making process
    91|- Product complexity and learning curve
    92|- Target market size and concentration
    93|
    94|### Step 2: Evaluate Market Conditions
    95|Assess your market dynamics:
    96|- Competitive intensity of your keywords/channels
    97|- Target audience location and accessibility
    98|- Budget availability for paid channels
    99|- Your team size and capabilities
   100|- Timeline to revenue generation
   101|
   102|### Step 3: Score Each Motion
   103|Rate fit for your product (1-10 scale):
   104|- Inbound: Content creation capability, brand building timeline
   105|- Outbound: Prospect list availability, sales team capacity
   106|- Paid: Budget flexibility, target audience clarity, conversion potential
   107|- Community: Existing communities, product network effects
   108|- Partners: Complementary products, channel availability
   109|- ABM: Deal size and account concentration
   110|- PLG: Product trial-ability, pricing flexibility
   111|
   112|### Step 4: Design Motion Stack
   113|Select and prioritize 2-4 motions to execute:
   114|- Primary motion (highest potential for your business)
   115|- Secondary motions (complementary acquisition channels)
   116|- Motion sequencing (which to start first)
   117|- Resource allocation across channels
   118|
   119|### Step 5: Build Execution Plan
   120|Create 90-day implementation roadmap:
   121|- Quick wins and early validation
   122|- Team and tool requirements
   123|- Success metrics for each motion
   124|- Optimization and scaling strategy
   125|- Budget and resource allocation
   126|
   127|## Input Format
   128|Use $ARGUMENTS to pass:
   129|- Product description and positioning
   130|- Target customer profile and market
   131|- Price point and sales cycle
   132|- Team size and capabilities
   133|- Budget and timeline constraints
   134|- Existing channels or data
   135|
   136|## Output
   137|A comprehensive GTM motions analysis including:
   138|- Scoring of all 7 motions for your product
   139|- Recommended motion stack (primary and secondary)
   140|- Tool recommendations for each motion
   141|- 90-day execution plan with milestones
   142|- Resource and budget requirements
   143|- Success metrics and measurement framework
   144|- Competitive differentiation through motion choice
   145|
   146|## Framework
   147|Based on Product Compass GTM motion analysis. Provides a systematic approach to balancing customer acquisition across multiple channels.
   148|
   149|## Tips
   150|- Most successful products use 2-4 complementary motions
   151|- Start with your strongest motion; add complexity gradually
   152|- Paid channels fund growth while organic channels build long-term value
   153|- Revisit motion mix quarterly as company scales
   154|- Combine inbound (brand) with outbound (sales) for B2B strength
   155|- Use PLG to reduce CAC; use paid to accelerate proven channels
