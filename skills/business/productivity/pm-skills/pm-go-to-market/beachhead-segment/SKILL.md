    14|# Beachhead Segment
    15|
    16|## Overview
    17|Identify the first beachhead market segment for product launch. This skill evaluates potential market segments against key criteria to find your initial winning segment that enables fast PMF validation and adjacent expansion.
    18|
    19|## When to Use
    20|- Choosing a first market for your product
    21|- Targeting an initial customer segment
    22|- Planning initial market entry strategy
    23|- Deciding where to focus limited resources
    24|- Validating GTM assumptions with early adopters
    25|
    26|## Key Evaluation Criteria
    27|
    28|### 1. Burning Pain Point
    29|Does this segment experience an acute, unmet problem?
    30|- Daily frustration with the status quo
    31|- Significant productivity loss or cost impact
    32|- Emotional urgency to find a solution
    33|- Current workarounds are expensive or fragile
    34|- Problem is getting worse over time
    35|
    36|### 2. Willingness to Pay
    37|Does this segment have budget and motivation to pay for a solution?
    38|- Documented budget allocation for this problem area
    39|- ROI is clear and compelling (value > cost)
    40|- Economic impact of problem justifies solution cost
    41|- Decision-maker has autonomy or influence over budget
    42|- No free or DIY alternatives that fully satisfy need
    43|
    44|### 3. Winnable Market Share
    45|Can you realistically capture 60-70% of this segment in 3-18 months?
    46|- Segment is large enough but not oversaturated
    47|- Limited competition or easy differentiation
    48|- Market players are fragmented or complacent
    49|- Your product has clear competitive advantage
    50|- You have unique access or distribution advantage
    51|
    52|### 4. Referral Potential
    53|Will customers naturally refer or recommend to others?
    54|- Segment contains professional communities
    55|- Customers interact with adjacent segments (expansion opportunity)
    56|- High word-of-mouth culture in this industry
    57|- Network effects within the segment
    58|- Solving problem for one creates demand in adjacent segments
    59|
    60|## How It Works
    61|
    62|### Step 1: List Potential Segments
    63|Brainstorm all possible target segments:
    64|- Industry verticals (SaaS, healthcare, manufacturing, etc.)
    65|- Company size (SMB, mid-market, enterprise)
    66|- Job titles or roles
    67|- Geographic regions
    68|- Use cases or use-case variations
    69|- Customer maturity level
    70|
    71|### Step 2: Research Pain Points
    72|Validate burning pain in each segment:
    73|- Customer interviews and discovery calls
    74|- Problem validation through surveys
    75|- Market research and analyst reports
    76|- Competitor positioning and customer reviews
    77|- Quantify cost/impact of the problem
    78|- Identify current workarounds and limitations
    79|
    80|### Step 3: Assess Willingness to Pay
    81|Determine budget and economic viability:
    82|- Segment's budget for this problem category
    83|- ROI calculation (value gained vs cost)
    84|- Current spending on solutions or workarounds
    85|- Budget decision-making process
    86|- Typical deal size expectations
    87|- Pricing sensitivity in the segment
    88|
    89|### Step 4: Evaluate Winnability
    90|Assess realistic market share potential:
    91|- Total addressable market (TAM) size
    92|- Competitive landscape and positioning
    93|- Your differentiation or unfair advantage
    94|- Distribution access to this segment
    95|- Time and resources required
    96|- Market growth and momentum
    97|
    98|### Step 5: Identify Referral Pathways
    99|Map expansion opportunities:
   100|- Adjacent segments that reference segment influences
   101|- Network effects within the segment
   102|- Professional communities and associations
   103|- Customer-to-customer recommendations
   104|- Natural expansion path to adjacent markets
   105|- Viral or network effects from solving core pain
   106|
   107|### Step 6: Select Beachhead
   108|Choose your primary launch segment:
   109|- Highest combined score across four criteria
   110|- Most achievable for your current resources
   111|- Shortest path to PMF and revenue
   112|- Best reference for adjacent expansion
   113|- Most enthusiastic early customer cohort
   114|
   115|## Input Format
   116|Use $ARGUMENTS to pass:
   117|- Product description and capabilities
   118|- Initial market research and validation data
   119|- Potential segment options
   120|- Constraints and limitations
   121|- Timeline and resource constraints
   122|- Current customer data or feedback
   123|
   124|## Output
   125|A beachhead segment analysis including:
   126|- Top 3-5 recommended segments with scoring
   127|- Primary beachhead segment recommendation
   128|- Pain point validation and evidence
   129|- Willingness to pay assessment and pricing guidance
   130|- Realistic market share and revenue projections
   131|- Referral and expansion pathways to adjacent segments
   132|- 90-day customer acquisition plan for beachhead
   133|- Post-beachhead expansion roadmap
   134|
   135|## Framework
   136|Based on Geoffrey Moore's beachhead market strategy in "Crossing the Chasm." Focuses on finding the smallest winnable, referenceable market that validates PMF and enables expansion.
   137|
   138|## Tips
   139|- Start absurdly specific. A niche beachhead is better than a vague mass market
   140|- Choose the segment most likely to evangelize your solution
   141|- Validate all four criteria with at least 10 customer interviews
   142|- Select segment with fastest path to revenue and references
   143|- Ensure beachhead can reference to adjacent market segments
   144|- Focus all resources on dominating the beachhead (not diluting efforts)
   145|- Plan exit from beachhead only after 60%+ market share
