    14|# Ideal Customer Profile
    15|
    16|## Overview
    17|Identify your Ideal Customer Profile (ICP) from research and survey data. This skill synthesizes customer research to define the customer most likely to find value, retain, and expand with your product.
    18|
    19|## When to Use
    20|- Defining ICP from product-market fit survey data
    21|- Targeting high-value customer segments
    22|- Analyzing customer success and expansion patterns
    23|- Prioritizing sales and marketing efforts
    24|- Evaluating new customer opportunities for fit
    25|- Refining target market definition
    26|
    27|## ICP Framework Components
    28|
    29|### Demographics
    30|Who are they from a firmographic and personal perspective?
    31|- Company size (employees, revenue)
    32|- Industry or vertical
    33|- Geographic location
    34|- Job title and department
    35|- Years of experience in role
    36|- Education and background
    37|- Organizational structure and reporting
    38|
    39|### Behaviors
    40|How do they work and make decisions?
    41|- How they discover and evaluate solutions
    42|- Buying process and decision-making timeline
    43|- Technical literacy and product adoption speed
    44|- Collaboration style (solo decision vs committee)
    45|- Change management and adoption style
    46|- Tool switching frequency
    47|- Community involvement and peer influence
    48|
    49|### Jobs to Be Done (JTBD)
    50|What are they trying to accomplish?
    51|- Primary job/goal they're trying to achieve
    52|- Secondary jobs that support the primary job
    53|- Emotional jobs (how they want to feel)
    54|- Social jobs (status and perception)
    55|- Jobs they avoid or want to eliminate
    56|- Frequency and importance of each job
    57|- Success metrics for completing job
    58|
    59|### Needs and Pain Points
    60|What problems does your product solve?
    61|- Specific pain points they experience
    62|- Current workarounds and limitations
    63|- Impact on productivity or outcomes
    64|- Cost or time burden of the problem
    65|- Emotional frustration levels
    66|- Barriers to solving the problem
    67|- Available budget to solve
    68|- Competing priorities
    69|
    70|## How It Works
    71|
    72|### Step 1: Gather Customer Data
    73|Collect research about actual and potential customers:
    74|- Product-market fit survey responses
    75|- Customer interview transcripts
    76|- Trial or freemium user behavior data
    77|- Customer feedback and support tickets
    78|- Churn analysis and customer lifecycle data
    79|- Win/loss analysis from sales
    80|- Competitor customer analysis
    81|
    82|### Step 2: Segment by Value
    83|Identify customer cohorts and their value:
    84|- Highest LTV (lifetime value) customers
    85|- Fastest time-to-value customers
    86|- Lowest churn rate customers
    87|- Highest expansion/upsell customers
    88|- Most enthusiastic/engaged customers
    89|- Best reference/case study potential
    90|- Most aligned with product vision
    91|
    92|### Step 3: Profile Demographics
    93|Extract firmographic patterns:
    94|- Common company sizes (employee count, revenue)
    95|- Industry verticals and sub-verticals
    96|- Geographic concentrations
    97|- Typical department and reporting structure
    98|- Budget holders and budget available
    99|- Company stage (startup, growth, enterprise)
   100|- Company culture indicators
   101|
   102|### Step 4: Identify Behaviors
   103|Map decision-making and adoption patterns:
   104|- How they discovered your product (channel)
   105|- Evaluation process and timeline
   106|- Key stakeholders in decision
   107|- Obstacles during sales process
   108|- Product adoption speed and breadth
   109|- Team involvement in onboarding
   110|- Frequency of feature usage
   111|- Support and service needs
   112|
   113|### Step 5: Define JTBD
   114|Articulate what they're trying to accomplish:
   115|- Primary job/goal (functional job)
   116|- Emotional dimensions (how they want to feel)
   117|- Social dimensions (team and stakeholder impact)
   118|- Success metrics (how they measure success)
   119|- Context and constraints (when, where, with whom)
   120|- Competing jobs and priorities
   121|- Importance ranking of various jobs
   122|
   123|### Step 6: Document Pain Points and Needs
   124|Synthesize specific problem areas:
   125|- Before state (current situation and frustrations)
   126|- Desired after state (ideal future state)
   127|- Gap size and impact quantification
   128|- Emotional dimensions of the problem
   129|- Resource constraints preventing solutions
   130|- Skepticism or hesitations
   131|- Success criteria for solution
   132|
   133|## Input Format
   134|Use $ARGUMENTS to pass:
   135|- Research data (surveys, interviews, transcripts)
   136|- Customer success/metrics data
   137|- Product usage analytics
   138|- Sales activity and win/loss data
   139|- Existing customer database
   140|- Competitive intelligence
   141|
   142|## Output
   143|A comprehensive ICP definition including:
   144|- Firmographic profile (company size, industry, location)
   145|- Behavioral profile (buying patterns, adoption style)
   146|- Complete JTBD mapping (functional, emotional, social jobs)
   147|- Top 5-7 pain points and specific needs
   148|- Quantified impact metrics (cost of problem, value of solution)
   149|- Decision-making process and key stakeholders
   150|- Typical customer journey and timeline
   151|- Go-to-market implications and messaging
   152|- Disqualification criteria (who is NOT a good fit)
   153|- High-value segment within ICP (ideal-of-the-ideal)
   154|
   155|## Framework
   156|Based on Jobs to Be Done theory by Clayton Christensen and customer profiling methodology. Combines behavioral data with motivational insights to define actionable customer profiles.
   157|
   158|## Tips
   159|- Use quantitative and qualitative data together
   160|- Interview 10+ high-value customers for pattern identification
   161|- Look for non-obvious demographic patterns (outliers can be high-value)
   162|- Define both ideal ICP and acceptable secondary segments
   163|- Revisit ICP quarterly as you gather more customer data
   164|- Use ICP to evaluate all new sales opportunities
   165|- Share ICP across entire organization (marketing, sales, product)
   166|- Remember: ICP should drive focus, not exclude all others
